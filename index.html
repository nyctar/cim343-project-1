The Darién Gap


Image from Joshua Collins, The New Humanitarian

In search of a better future, thousands of migrants flee their countries to escape repression and risk their lives to cross the Darién Gap. The 97-kilometer stretch of jungle is the only overland route that connects the Americas, with most migrants hoping to reach the US-Mexico border. Along the way, incredible dangers, both natural and human, await.

You are one of them.

Having recently arrived in Acandí, Colombia, the starting point, you must now continue your journey by trekking through the treacherous terrain of the Darién Gap to reach Bajo Chiquito, Panama.

Text

The End

The Darién Gap is just a small but harrowing part of the journey migrants take in search of a better future. The jungle is unforgiving, with dangerous terrain and wildlife. But an even greater dangers lurk—robbery, smuggling, human trafficking, physical and sexual violence—driven by lawlessness and exploitation. Despite these threats, thousands still choose to cross with desperation and hope.

Based on stories reported in the region, this game reflects only a fraction of the harsh decisions and risks real migrants must endure to survive. It is merely a rough approximation, unable to fully capture the gravity of their struggle.

Story based on 'The invisibles: A Cuban asylum seeker’s dangerous odyssey,' The New Humanitarian, 2022, and 'Echoes from the jungle: The unseen human stories of the Darien Gap,' International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies, 2022.
